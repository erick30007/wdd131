Famous Landmarks

Angkor Wat: Siem Reap, Cambodia

The ancient temples spread across Angkor Archaeological Park constitute one of Southeast Asia's most significant archaeological sites. This UNESCO World Heritage Site showcases the remains of the capital of the Khmer Empire from the ninth to 15th centuries, including the world's largest religious monument: the expansive Angkor Wat temple complex. As you explore the rest of Angkor, the Bayon temple at Angkor Thom, which sits north of the main complex, is also not to be missed. If you want to manage your own visit, it's easily accessible by taxi, tuktuk or even bicycle from the city of Siem Reap, as the park is on the edge of the city.

Sydney Opera House: Sydney

The Sydney Opera House is one of the world's top tourist attractions and arguably Australia's most recognizable building. Located right alongside Sydney Harbour and its famed bridge, this landmark is also known as one of the busiest performing arts venues in the world, hosting operas, orchestras, ballet, theater and more. To get to know the famous Sydney Opera House, you can take the hourlong guided Sydney Opera House Tour. Additional guided options are also available, including a comprehensive backstage tour of the venue. Afterward, stay for drinks or dinner harborside at one of the venue's indoor or outdoor restaurants.

Eiffel Tower: Paris

The Eiffel Tower is one of the most visited monuments in the world , so you may want to plan a visit during one of the shoulder seasons spring or fall to avoid the crowds. It's cheaper to only visit the second level of the tower, and you can save even more if you want to walk nearly 700 stairs up to it instead of taking the elevator. You can even dine within the Eiffel Tower at Madame Brasserie or at the Michelin-starred Jules Verne, which has a mandatory elegant dress code. After sunset, you'll see why this must-visit destination in France is known as the City of Light: The Eiffel Tower puts on its own dazzling light show at the top of each hour after dark.
